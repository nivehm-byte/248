The Problem

Most ministry models were not built for cities

Urban populations are growing faster than any organisation can plan for. Most traditional mission approaches share four structural weaknesses.

01

Too Slow

Cities grow faster than trained professionals can keep up. Our systems cannot match the pace of urbanisation.

02

Too Expensive

Buildings, salaries and programs create overhead that limits how far the work can go.

03

Too Centralised

Growth depends on outside leaders and funding to function. When they leave, momentum leaves with them.

04

Too Fragile

When the context changes, the movement stalls. Local ownership was never built.

The issue is not commitment. It is a model that does not consistently develop local leaders who can develop others.

The Answer

Multiplication, not addition

2 → 4 → 8 → 16 → 32…

The only way to reach cities is not to add — one program at a time — but to multiply. We develop leaders, who invest in other leaders, who multiply Jesus movements in cities. We don't launch programs. We multiply the multipliers.

Who We Are

Developing leaders who multiply Jesus movements in the cities of the world

We find local leaders already positioned to start movements in their cities. We invest in them. We coach them. And when the movement no longer needs us, we step back.

The Model

Every leader we invest in receives three things

We act as catalyst and coach. We ignite what could not start without us — then step back as local ownership grows.

A Movement Guide

An experienced practitioner who walks alongside the leader long-term — coaching through real challenges on the ground.

A Growth Environment

Communities of practice — cohorts, cross-city learning, and exposure to other movement leaders that stretch vision and sharpen work.

Developmental Content

A structured coaching pathway covering team health, mission clarity, outreach, and leader multiplication. Practical tools, not academic curriculum.
